. What a "Model" actually is (The Math Equation)
When the AI says "We will train a Linear Regression model", don't imagine a robot.
Imagine a simple math equation you learned in school: Y = mX + b (The equation for a straight line).

X = The features (e.g., the number of rooms in a house).

m = The "weight" or "slope" (how much does price go up for each extra room?).

b = The "intercept" (the base price if there were zero rooms).

Y = The predicted price.

Training a model simply means: "Find the perfect 'm' and 'b' numbers so that the line fits the housing data as closely as possible."
(Your model will have hundreds of these 'm' values because it looks at many features, not just rooms—but the idea is exactly the same).
